# The BitBooks Book — Table of Contents & Charter

**Version:** 0.1.0 (skeleton) · **Pattern:** RUNSTR Book (parts-by-pillar)
**Record. Close. Prove.**

> **Charter — how this book gets written.** RUNSTR's book documents a *shipped* app, and its credibility comes from that. BitBooks is pre-launch, so this book is written **milestone by milestone**: each chapter is drafted in the same quarter its subject ships, never ahead of it. The TOC below is the commitment; empty chapters stay visibly empty ("ships with M2") rather than being padded with aspiration. The book is public-facing and doubles as the deep half of build-in-public.

## Quick Reference (stable now)

**The Three Pillars:** Record (double-entry + native lots) · Prove (manifest, close, Package, exports) · Suite (one Recovery Key, ₿LOC bridge).
**Core concepts:** Three truths, never blended · live for display, confirmed for records · lots written, never recomputed · unknown basis is loud · templates are the front door · COMPLETE or DEGRADED, nothing between · free means free · records, not advice.
**Reading order for newcomers:** 00 → 01 → 12 (the artifact everything serves) → back to Part 1.
